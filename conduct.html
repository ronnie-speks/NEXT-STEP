Ethics & Standards

Code of Conduct

Our Commitment to Ethical Practice

Every member of the Next Step Initiative — staff, volunteers, committee members, and partners — is bound by these principles, which reflect our deepest commitment to the students and communities we serve.

Article 01

The Student-First Principle

Every decision made within the Next Step Initiative is guided by one overriding question: what is best for the student? The welfare, dignity, and development of the students we serve is the foundation of everything we do.

  • All guidance and counseling must prioritize the holistic well-being and long-term interests of the student above all other considerations.
  • No activity, partnership, or resource shall be pursued if it compromises the safety, dignity, or development of any student.
  • Students must be treated as active participants in their career journeys — their voices, interests, and choices must always be respected.
  • Sessions must be age-appropriate, culturally sensitive, and adapted to the specific contexts of P7, S4, and S6 learners.
Article 02

Professional Integrity & Excellence

All members of the Next Step Initiative are expected to uphold the highest standards of professional conduct and competence in every interaction with students, schools, and partners.

  • Represent qualifications, roles, and affiliations truthfully and accurately at all times.
  • Deliver career guidance sessions grounded in accurate, up-to-date information about professions and educational pathways.
  • Engage in continuous learning and professional development relevant to career counseling and youth guidance.
  • Avoid making promises or guarantees to students about career outcomes that cannot be substantiated.
  • Seek peer support and consultation in cases requiring specialized expertise beyond one's scope.
Article 03

Confidentiality & Trust

Students must feel safe to share their thoughts, fears, and aspirations freely. The confidential nature of counseling interactions is a cornerstone of effective guidance.

  • All personal information shared by students during individual counseling sessions must be kept strictly confidential.
  • Information may only be shared with school authorities or guardians where required by law or to protect the student's safety.
  • Students must be clearly informed at the outset about the nature and limits of confidentiality.
  • Records and data collected must be stored securely and used only for the purposes of improving guidance services.
Article 04

Inclusivity & Non-Discrimination

The Next Step Initiative is committed to reaching every student in the Lango sub-region, regardless of gender, ethnicity, religion, disability, or socioeconomic background.

  • Programs must be designed and delivered to ensure equitable participation of students from both rural and urban schools.
  • No student shall be denied access to guidance or counseling services on the basis of any personal characteristic.
  • All communication and sessions must be delivered in an accessible and culturally affirming manner.
  • Particular attention must be paid to girls and marginalized groups who may face additional barriers to career exploration.
  • Any discriminatory behavior by members or volunteers will be subject to immediate review and disciplinary action.
Article 05

Responsible Partnerships

Strategic partnerships are central to the sustainability and reach of the Next Step Initiative. All partnerships must align with and uphold our values and the interests of the students we serve.

  • All partnerships must be formalized through clear agreements outlining roles, responsibilities, and expectations.
  • Partners must demonstrate a commitment to student welfare and the ethical standards of this initiative.
  • The initiative will not accept support from organizations whose activities conflict with the well-being of children and youth.
  • Mentors and visiting professionals must be vetted, briefed on conduct expectations, and monitored during engagements with students.
Article 06

Accountability & Impact

The Next Step Initiative holds itself accountable to the students, schools, and communities it serves, as well as to its funders and partners. Transparency and evidence of impact are non-negotiable.

  • The initiative will maintain accurate records of all school visits, counseling sessions, and student interactions.
  • Regular monitoring and evaluation will be conducted to assess the impact on students' career decision-making.
  • Financial resources will be managed transparently, with clear reporting to all funders and stakeholders.
  • Feedback from students, schools, and communities will be actively sought and used to improve program delivery.
  • Any violations of this Code of Conduct will be investigated and addressed promptly and impartially.

Our Pledge to Every Student

"We commit to serving you with honesty, respect, and genuine care — because your future matters, and guiding you toward it is our highest responsibility."
